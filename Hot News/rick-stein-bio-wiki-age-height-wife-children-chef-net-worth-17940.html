Rick Stein Biography

Christopher Richard “Rick” Stein, CBE is an English celebrity chef, restaurateur, and television presenter. He was born and brought up in Oxfordshire, England. He has run the Stein hotel and restaurant with his first wife Jill Stein.

Rick is also the head chef and a co-owner of “Rick Stein at Bannisters” at Mollymook and Port Stephens in Australia, with his second wife, Sarah. He has written cookery books and presented television programs.

Rick Stein Chef

Rick After graduating, he converted a mobile disco in Padstow, which he had run as a student, into a quayside nightclub with his friend, Johnny. It became known for its freeze-dried curries. However, the nightclub lost its license and was closed down by the police, mainly due to frequent brawls with local fishermen. The pair still had a license for a restaurant in another part of the building, so they continued with that to avert bankruptcy.

Stein ran the kitchen using the experience he had gained as a commis chef. Eventually, he converted it into a small harbor-side bistro, “The Seafood Restaurant”, with his first wife Jill in 1975.

As of 2015, his business operates four restaurants, a bistro, a café, a seafood delicatessen, a pâtisserie shop, a gift shop, and a cookery school. In 2007 threats against Stein’s businesses were made by Cornish nationalists. His impact on the economy of Padstow is such that it has been nicknamed “Padstein”.

Rick Stein’s Café in Padstow

In 2018, Rick opened up a second “Stein at Bannisters” in Salamander Bay in Port Stephens, with his second wife Sarah Stein. Stein has become a popular television presenter on food programs.

After appearing once as a guest chef in Keith Floyd’s 1985 series Floyd on Fish and in his 1986 series Floyd on Food, he was offered the chance to present his own series – like the “travelogue” style of cookery show pioneered by Floyd – on BBC television, using Floyd’s producer and director David Pritchard.
